Biography of John Tutu Adeklo (Dordor)

Hunter, Pioneer Presbyter, and Founding Pillar of the Church in Hlefi

Early Life and Ancestry

In the oral memory of the people of Hlefi, John Tutu Adeklo—also known by the family name Dordor—is remembered as a man born into a generation when life in the Akwapim–Togo mountain settlements demanded strength, endurance, and wisdom.

He grew up in the rural Ewe environment of what is today the Ho West area of Ghana. Like many men of his time, he lived close to nature and mastered the skills needed for survival in the forested hills around Hlefi and its surrounding communities. From an early age, he became known as a skilled and respected hunter, providing food and contributing to the wellbeing of his community.

Life as a Hunter and Community Figure

Before formal Christianity fully rooted itself in Hlefi, John Tutu Adeklo was already known as a dependable figure in the village. His hunting expeditions across the Akwapim–Togo ranges provided meat and resources for families and strengthened his reputation as a courageous and disciplined man.

In oral tradition, he is remembered not only as a hunter but as a man of deep respect for spiritual matters, living a life that later aligned naturally with the teachings of the early Christian missionaries.

Marriage and Family Life

Tutu & Ruth

John Tutu Adeklo was married to Ruth Ehah, an industrious and hardworking woman originally from Todome in the Volta Region. She is remembered in family tradition as a strong partner who contributed greatly to the stability and growth of their household.

Together, John Tutu Adeklo and Ruth Ehah were blessed with seven children, forming the foundation of the Adeklo (Dordor) family lineage. Their home became a center of discipline, hard work, and early Christian values that would influence future generations.

Conversion and Rise in the Early Church

With the arrival and spread of the Evangelical Presbyterian Church in Hlefi, John Tutu Adeklo became one of the earliest converts in the community. His integrity, leadership among hunters, and respected standing in the village made him a natural leader in the emerging Christian congregation.

The first Presbytery leader (Presbyter) of the E.P. Church in Hlefi

  • Supporting missionary catechists
  • Organizing early worship gatherings
  • Visiting and encouraging new converts
  • Maintaining discipline and unity among believers

Founding Role in the Hlefi Church

As one of the earliest indigenous church leaders in Hlefi, John Tutu Adeklo played a foundational role in shaping the spiritual life of the community. In the early E.P. Church system, presbyters were not only spiritual leaders but also custodians of order and moral guidance. His leadership helped stabilize the church during its formative years.

Character and Legacy

  • A disciplined and fearless hunter
  • A committed early Christian leader
  • A respected elder of Hlefi
  • A family man who raised seven children with Ruth Ehah
  • A foundational pillar of the E.P. Church in Hlefi

His life reflects the transition of a generation that moved from traditional livelihoods into structured Christian community leadership.

Final Years and Remembrance

Although written historical records are limited, oral tradition holds that John Tutu Adeklo lived to witness the strengthening of the church he helped establish and the growth of his children within the Christian faith. He is remembered as part of the pioneering generation that shaped both the spiritual and social foundation of Hlefi.
